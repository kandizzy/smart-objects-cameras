# A Moment in the Room: NodCheck

**Date:** Monday evening, March 2026, ~6-7pm
**Location:** Smart Objects class, SVA

It's a Monday evening in March, probably around 6 or 7pm. We're in Smart Objects and Carrie, our instructor, is going through a lesson on ambient and invisible tech, the kind of technological systems that sense without being seen. The room is quiet in that way where you can tell nobody's really following along.

She scans the room, notices all the blank stares, and asks if it makes sense. Nobody says anything. I vigorously shake my head no. She immediately perks up and genuinely thanks me for being honest. "I had a feeling people were lost from all the blank stares!"

From that point on, other students start speaking up and admitting they're lost too. She slows down, reexplains, and starts asking more specific questions about whether certain terms or processes are familiar. The second pass actually lands.

## Why this moment mattered

The whole room was confused the entire time, but it took one person shaking their head to make that visible. That's what inspired NodCheck. There should be a low-risk way for a student to let the teacher know they're lost. And the fact that this happened during a lesson about invisible sensing is a little ironic, because that's exactly what the tool is trying to do.
