# Vignette — Sleep Detection

**Students:** Kevin Shi · Mingyue Zhou
**Project:** Sleep Detection
**Camera used:** webcam / laptop

---

## The moment

I remember, one time during a guest talk session, I found myself slowly fall asleep. It wasn't that I wasn't interested, the topic was actually interesting, but I had slept late the night before, and I just couldn't stay fully awake. I remember trying to focus, but my eyes kept closing for a few seconds at a time.

At one point, I realized I had completely missed a key explanation. Everyone around me was still following along, and I felt a bit frustrated because I knew I had lost track, but I didn't want to interrupt the talk or ask the guest speaker to repeat it.

What made it more uncomfortable was that I didn't want the professor to call me out. If they noticed me and said my name, it would feel embarrassing in front of everyone. At the same time, I did wish someone could have helped me in that moment. If a friend sitting next to me had quietly nudged me or whispered me. I think that would have been the best kind of reminder, something small and supportive, without drawing attention.

## Why this moment mattered

This moment made me realize that staying awake in class is not just about discipline and it's also about how reminders are delivered. There is a big difference between being publicly corrected and being quietly supported.

It also shaped how I think about my project. Instead of designing a system that directly calls out individuals, I started thinking about how a system could re-engage attention without causing embarrassment, and how it might feel more like a shared or ambient reminder.
