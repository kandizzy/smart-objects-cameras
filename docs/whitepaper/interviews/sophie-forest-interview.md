# Outsider Interview: Voice Forest

**Student / interviewer:** Sophie
**Project:** Voice Forest
**Camera used:** sound detection only
**Interviewee:** Mom — my mother, not in any design or tech field
**Their relationship to the class:** none
**Date of interview:** May 2, 2026
**Format:** video call
**Length:** ~10 minutes

---

## Why this person?

My mom is the most direct person I know. She doesn't soften feedback or try to be polite about things she doesn't understand. That makes her a good test for whether something actually makes sense to an outsider, or whether I've been assuming too much context.

## What you showed them

I opened the demo during a video call without much of the setup. I didn't explain what it was for or how it worked, but just showed it running and ask questions freely. 

---

## The conversation

### 1. Before I tell you anything — what do you think this is doing?

> When are you supposed to talk?

### 2. What do you think it's *for*? Who would use it?

> Doesn't it need people's attention to sustain it? If it runs on sound, and students keep talking, wouldn't that make the classroom really noisy?

### 3. What was confusing or unclear?

> Where is this being shown?

### 4. What surprised you — good or bad?

> Isn't it just like the little spirits in a Miyazaki forest? Like they just appeared?

### 5. If you walked into a room with this running, would you even notice it? Would you want to?

> I think it would be kind of funny to have this in a classroom.(laughing)

### 6. What would make this feel creepy or invasive? What would make it feel useful?

> Doesn't it need people looking at it to mean anything?

### 7. What's missing? What did you expect it to do that it doesn't?

>What if the classroom lights are too bright? How would you even see the forest?

### 8. If you walked into a classroom and saw this forest on the wall — how would that make you feel?

> A little funny, but also kind of special.

---

## What I learned that I couldn't have learned from a classmate

What surprised me most was that she found it funny — not in a dismissive way, but in the way you laugh when something is unexpectedly alive. I hadn't thought about the comedic quality of trees appearing in a classroom. She also immediately flagged the lighting problem: if the room lights are on, you can't see the projection. That's a practical issue I'd mentally set aside, but she caught it in seconds. And her concern about students talking making the class noisy revealed a cultural assumption I hadn't examined — she sees student voice as disruption, not contribution. The forest treats all voices equally, which felt meaningful to me, but she read it as potentially chaotic.

## One quote worth keeping

> Isn't it just like the little spirits in a Miyazaki forest? Like they just appeared?

— *Mom*

## What you'd change based on this

Her lighting concern is something worth considering -> the projection needs a dark enough environment to be visible, which limits where and when this works. I'd also think about whether the forest could identify individual voices, so it responds differently to the teacher versus students, and it might address her concern about noise becoming the input.
